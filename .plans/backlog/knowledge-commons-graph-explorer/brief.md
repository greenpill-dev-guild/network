# Knowledge Commons Graph Explorer

**Stage**: `backlog`

## Why This Exists

The first public graph explorer proved that Greenpill has enough network and podcast data to make relationships visible, but it shipped ahead of the stewardship model needed for a public knowledge commons. The Regen Knowledge Commons Toolkit argues that public graphs need source lineage, maturity language, relationship grammar, review paths, and public-use boundaries before they become discovery infrastructure.

The graph should move out of the public website for now and become a planned living artifact: a source-aware Resource Graph and Ecosystem Atlas for Greenpill's chapters, guilds, people, projects, podcast ecosystem, and adjacent regen media.

## Desired Outcome

Create a future implementation plan for a Knowledge Commons Graph Explorer that can eventually return as a public or semi-public interface when the data is reviewed, stewarded, and safe to publish.

The future explorer should help people understand:

- how Greenpill chapters, guilds, projects, stewards, and partners relate;
- how Greenpill Podcast, Ma Earth, and Crypto Altruists map overlapping regen and web3 fields;
- what forms of capital, knowledge, credibility, and coordination move between nodes;
- which entries are sourced, reviewed, experimental, sensitive, or not ready for public use.

## Non-Goals

- Do not ship a public explorer in the current website release.
- Do not serve graph JSON from `public/data`.
- Do not adopt a graph database before relationship grammar and stewardship capacity are clear.
- Do not publish people maps, indigenous knowledge, local chapter details, or relationship claims without review and public-use boundaries.
