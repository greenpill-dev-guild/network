# Greenpill V2 Steward Brief
Prepared for the V2 steward decision pass

## Why now
The Charmverse transition makes this the moment to make Greenpill easier to understand, remove public dead ends, and create a dependable home for steward work. The goal is not a full redesign. It is to lock the few choices that most affect the public story, onboarding experience, and first impression.

## What we heard
- The current site already has real strengths, especially the map, the explorer, and the sense that Greenpill is a living network.
- Newcomers need clearer language and clearer next steps. People should be able to understand what Greenpill is and where to begin without inside context.
- The human layer matters. Stewards, chapters, and visible examples of real activity make onboarding more trustworthy.
- The strongest near-term direction is continuity first: simplify, clarify, and remove public dependency risk now, then deepen the experience after the transition.

## What is already aligned
- Keep the existing Greenpill feel and strengthen it rather than rebrand.
- Make the public site easier for newcomers while keeping member and steward work in a separate workspace.
- Replace visible public Charmverse paths with Greenpill-owned pages.
- Start with a practical working home for steward work instead of trying to rebuild everything at once.

## What needs steward input now
- **Homepage promise:** what should a newcomer understand in the first 30 seconds?
- **Proof on the homepage:** should we lead with chapter stories, builder work, or current programs?
- **Greenpill Garden framing:** should it appear as a flagship program or as one part of a broader network story?
- **Flagship examples:** which chapters, guilds or pods, and builder projects are strong enough to feature now?

## What happens next
Please read this brief, then respond to the smaller Telegram question set. We only need to lock the few open narrative choices and gather nominations for what should be featured first.
